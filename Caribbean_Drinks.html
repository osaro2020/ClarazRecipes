Classic Margarita

Servings

1

Ingredients

1 ½ oz Tequila
½ oz Triple Sec
1 oz fresh lime juice or lemon juice
Salt (Optional)

Directions

Shake all ingredients and cracked ice into a shaker.

Rim the margarita glass or any other type of glass with salt (if desired).

Then, pour the mix into the margarita glass or any glass of choice.

Frozen Strawberry Margarita

Servings

1

Ingredients

¼ cup sliced fresh strawberries or frozen strawberries
1 ½ oz Tequila
½ oz Triple Sec
1 oz fresh lime juice or lemon juice
1 tsp white sugar (optional)
Salt (optional)

Directions

Blend all ingredients into blender for approximately 10 seconds. Add ice cubes, blend for an additional 15 seconds.

Rim the margarita glass or any other type of glass with salt (if desired).

Pour the mix into the margarita glass or any glass of choice.

Non-alcoholic Pina Colada

Servings

2-3

Ingredients

2 cups pineapple juice
¾ cup cream of coconut
Ice
Pineapple wedges (optional)
Whipped cream (optional)

Directions

Blend the pineapple juice and cream of coconut for 15 seconds. Add ice, then blend for 30 seconds or more until smooth.

Pour into glasses, top with whipped cream, then garnish with the pineapple wedges.

Pina Colada

Servings

2-3

Ingredients

3 oz white rum
3 oz coconut cream (ex: Coco Lopez)
3 oz pineapple juice
¼ cup chopped fresh pineapple fruits or frozen pineapples
2 cups ice
2 oz golden rum
Pineapple wedges (optional)
Whipped Cream (optional)

Directions

Blend the white rum, coconut cream, pineapple juice, pineapple fruits and ice for 15 seconds or until smooth.

Pour into glasses stir in the golden rum (or leave the golden rum to float on top). Add whipped cream (if desired), then garnish with the pineapple wedges.

Barbados Rum Punch

Servings

4

Ingredients

¼ cup gold rum (such as Mount Gay)
2/3 cup pineapple-coconut juice (such as R. W. Knudsen)
¼ cup fresh lime juice
6 tbsp Ginger Ale
Fresh lime or pineapple slice

Directions

Combine the first 3 ingredients in a large pitcher. Cover, and chill until ready to serve.

Divide rum mixture among 4 ice-filled glasses. Stir 1 ½ tablespoons ginger ale into each serving.

Garnish with fresh lime or pineapple slices, if desired.

Tip:

If you can’t find Mount Gay Rum made in Barbados just use your favorite gold or amber rum. Be sure to purchase a good-quality, low-sugar ginger ale, such as Fever-Tree, because the kick of spice works with lime to balance the sweetness.

Caribbean Carrot Punch

Servings

8

Ingredients

2 ¼ lb carrots
4 ½ cup water
1 ¼ cup sweetened condensed milk
1 ½ tsp vanilla
½ tsp cinnamon
½ tsp nutmeg

Directions

Clean the carrots, chop them and put into a food processor or blender, add water, blend until pureed. (it is best to do this in two batches).

Strain into a bowl using a fine strainer. You can strain a second time if you prefer less pulp in the juice.

Add the vanilla, cinnamon and nutmeg and stir well.

Add the sweetened condensed milk as desired. You can start with half a tin and add until it is sweet enough.

Chill for a couple of hours and serve over ice.

Mai Tai Cocktail

Servings

1-2

Ingredients

1 - 1 ½ cup ice, depends on taste (drink should not taste diluted)
2 oz light rum
2 oz gold rum
1 oz orange curacao liqueur
1 oz orgeat syrup
2 oz lime juice
1 - 1 ½ cup ice, depends on taste
2 oz dark rum
2 pineapple wedges
2 cherries

Directions

Shake all the ingredients into a shaker except the additional 1 ½ cup of ice (set that aside).

Pour into a glass, preferably an old-fashioned glass with the 1 ½ cup of ice you just set aside. The add the mixture to the glasses.

Garnish with the pineapple and cherry.

Non-Alcholic Berry Mojito

Servings

1

Ingredients

3 - 4 fresh raspberries
2 lime wedges
5 leaves of fresh mint
2 - 3 teaspoons sugar
Crushed ice

Directions

Add the berries, the fresh mint and sugar in a blender, blend thoroughly.

Pour the mixture into 8 - 12 oz glass, add the crushed ice, a splash of lime juice and club soda to fill the glass.

Then shake well and garnish with a lime wedge & additional mint. Serve and enjoy!

Saoco - Refreshing Rum Drink

Servings

1

Ingredients

2 ½ oz coconut water
1 ½ oz light rum
2 teaspoons fresh lime juice
2 teaspoon sugar
Ice cubes

Directions

Add all the ingredients together, with the exception of the ice in a shaker glass.

Stir with a spoon until the sugar dissolves.

Pour the mixture into a stemmed cocktail glass filled with ice.

Serve and Enjoy.

Sangria Criolla (Caribbean Sangria)

Servings

3-4

Ingredients

½ liter rose wine
½ cup rum
¼ cup pineapple juice
½ cup lemon juice
½ lemon (sliced into rounds)
½ lime (sliced into rounds)
1 mango (peeled and cut into chunks and divide into two)
½ cup sugar (optional)

Directions

Pour into the large glass pitcher, the wine, rum, pineapple juice, lemon juice and sugar. Stir well.

Now, add the sliced lemons, limes, and the mango.

Place the pitcher in the refrigerator and chill.

Tip:

Save some of the lemon or lime peel for garnish. Cool before serving.

Jamaican Rum Punch

Servings

5

Ingredients

½ cup lime or lemon juice
1 cup Jamaican white over-proof rum (Appleton or other simple syrup)
1 ½ cup white over-proof rum
1 strawberry flavored syrup (or other simple red)
1 ½ cup strawberry flavored syrup (or other simple red colored syrup)
2 cups water

Directions

Mix everything together in a punch bowl. Garnish the mixture with slices of lime and/or pieces of pineapple.

Chill in the refrigerator. The longer it stays in the refrigerator, the better it tastes, so serve after 2 hours.

Serve cold on ice.

Nigerian Chapman

Servings

2

Ingredients

2 (75 ml) tall cocktail glasses
17 cl (165ml) Fanta (chilled)
17 cl (165ml) Sprite (chilled)
2 to 2 ½ tbsp Ribena or Blackcurrant or Grenadine syrup (this is what gives the Chapman its unique ''reddish-pink'' color)
A quarter tsp Angostura aromatic bitters
7 to 10 ice cubes (or more)
1 whole cucumber
5 slices of both lemon and oranges

Directions

First, slice the lemon, orange and cucumber into rounds or quarters and set aside.

Pour into a jug or bowl, the ice cubes, add the Fanta and Sprite and mix together.

Then, add a tbsp of Grenadine syrup or Ribena or blackcurrant.

Add the Angostura aromatic bitters, mix thoroughly.

Finally, add the lemon and orange slices, cool in the refrigerator before serving. Add some more ice, serve in a cocktail glass (with a straw if desired). Use the sliced cucumber to garnish the edge of each glass.

Tips:

Don’t add the ice if not going to be taking immediately because it will make the drink taste flat quickly. The fizzy (effervescence) from the carbonated drinks (Fanta, Sprite) adds to the unique taste of the drink and if left for a while, the drinks will lose the taste, also adding to the flatness.

Add the Angostura aromatic bitters drop by drop, until you get your desired taste. To prepare Nigerian Chapman for kids you can omit the angostura bitters because it contains 45% alcohol. In the absence of Angostura bitters, use Peychaud’s bitters or Orange bitters.
